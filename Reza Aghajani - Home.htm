textbook

The required textbook for this course is

  • S. M. Ross, A First Course In Probability . Pearson Education, 9th Edition, 2014.

Homework problems will be assigned from this book, so you need to have the correct edition of the textbook from the first week.

Further (optional) references are:

  • R. Durret, Elementary Probability for Applications . Cambridge University Press, 2009.
  • L. Breiman, Probability . SIAM, 1968.

Course Policy and Academic Integrity

  • Make-up Exams. Make-up midterm exams will not be given. If you miss a midterm exam because of extraordinary circumstances, such as a serious illness or family emergency, and document the circumstances, then you may substitute your final exam score for the missed exam. In such cases, you must notify the instructor as soon as possible.
  • Late Homework. Solutions will be posted soon after the deadline, so late homework will not be accepted. Accommodations will be made only under extraordinary circumstances.
  • Homework Regrade Request. Your graded homework assignments will be returned to you during your discussion sections. You should tell your TA about your regrade request before leaving the section classroom.
  • Exam Regrade Requests. Regrade requests on exams will be handled through Gradescope. Regrade requests are intended to correct possible errors in the grading; grading rubric will not be changed, and "I deserve more partial credits'' requests will not be accepted.
  • Academic Integrity. Student are expected to adhere to the UCSD Policy on Integrity of Scholarship . Violations will be reported to the Academic Integrity Coordinator. Students are permitted to discuss concepts behind homework problems with each other, and may consult the instructor and the TAs, however, each student should write homework solutions independently. Any such discussion, as well as any consultation with written sources (other than the textbook and lecture notes) should be explicitly acknowledged. Students are not allowed to obtain solution manuals, or make use of web sites/online forums that help students with homework problems.
